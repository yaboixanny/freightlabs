Scaling a Regional Freight Brokerage with High-Intent Google Ads

How we helped a family-owned brokerage generate 3–5 qualified shipper leads per day by targeting high-value freight lanes.

Client Overview

A regional, family-owned freight brokerage wanted to grow beyond referrals and inconsistent outbound sales. They needed predictable inbound shipper demand without wasting budget on low-quality freight inquiries.

The Challenge

Before partnering with Freight Labs, the brokerage struggled with several key inhibitors to growth:

  • Unpredictable monthly lead volume
  • Heavy reliance on referrals and cold outreach
  • Wasted ad spend on broad freight searches
  • Low conversion rates from generic campaigns
  • Difficulty attracting profitable lane opportunities

Their sales team needed consistent, qualified shipper conversations to fill their pipeline and scale operations.

Our Strategy

We rebuilt their acquisition system around high-intent Google search traffic, focusing only on shippers actively moving freight. We didn't want traffic; we wanted shippers.

1) Lane-Specific Campaign Targeting

Instead of targeting generic, expensive searches like "freight broker" or "shipping company," we targeted specific lanes such as:

  • FTL shipping Texas to Illinois
  • Chicago to Dallas freight service
  • Midwest LTL freight shipping
  • Dedicated shipping lanes searches

2) Conversion-Focused Landing Pages

We built lane-focused landing pages designed to convert traffic into quote requests instantly. Each page emphasized regional lane expertise, fast response quoting, and carrier capacity reliability.

3) Intent Filtering & Negative Keywords

We filtered out non-buyer searches (like driver jobs or parcel shipping) to ensure every dollar was spent on potential shippers.

Business Impact

The brokerage moved from unpredictable demand to a scalable inbound acquisition engine. This allowed them to:

  • Expand carrier partnerships with confidence
  • Increase lane density and profit margins
  • Plan hiring based on data, not guesses
  • Reduce reliance on grueling cold outbound sales
"In freight brokerage marketing, specific intent beats broad traffic every time. Shippers searching for exact lanes consistently convert better than generic freight traffic."
